The Story of Butter Chicken: A Moti Mahal Signature

Published on September 11, 2025 by Niranjan Ramamurthy

Certain dishes are more than just food; they are a piece of culinary history, a story of innovation served on a plate. Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is one such dish, and its journey from a clever solution to a global sensation is directly tied to the legacy of Moti Mahalâ€”the renowned restaurant housed within Surya Plaza Hotel.

The Birth of the Tandoor Revolution

The story begins not with Butter Chicken, but with its predecessor: Tandoori Chicken. In the 1920s, in Peshawar (now in Pakistan), a young chef named Kundan Lal Gujral revolutionized cooking by experimenting with the traditional tandoor. While tandoors were commonly used for baking bread, Gujral was the first to use the clay oven for cooking chicken, creating the succulent, smoky Tandoori Chicken that would become famous worldwide.

However, this innovation presented a challenge. The skewered, tandoor-roasted chickens tended to dry out if not sold the same day. This practical problem of preventing waste set the stage for a stroke of culinary genius.

A Sauce Born of Necessity

To rehydrate the leftover chicken pieces, Gujral developed a rich, velvety gravy. This sauce, which he called "makhani" (buttery), was a masterful combination of tomatoes, fresh butter, and cream, balanced with a blend of subtle, aromatic spices. He would simmer the tandoori chicken in this makhani sauce, and in doing so, created an entirely new dish. The slight char of the tandoori chicken infused the creamy tomato gravy with a depth of flavor that was simply irresistible. Murgh Makhani was born.

From Delhi to the World Stage

Following the partition of India in 1947, Kundan Lal Gujral relocated to Delhi and established the first Moti Mahal restaurant in Daryaganj. It was here that Butter Chicken, along with another of his brilliant inventions, Dal Makhani, became legendary. The restaurant quickly gained acclaim, attracting dignitaries and heads of state from around the world, including India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, and U.S. President John F. Kennedy.

Experience the Authentic Legacy at Surya Plaza

At the Moti Mahal Tandoori Trail at Surya Plaza Hotel, we are proud custodians of this culinary tradition. Our Butter Chicken is prepared following the original principles that made it a global icon, offering you an authentic taste of a dish that has defined North Indian cuisine. It is more than a meal; it's an opportunity to experience a piece of history, served right here in Sahibganj.
