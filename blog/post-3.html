Designing for Comfort: The Making of Our Suites

Published on September 11, 2025 by Niranjan Ramamurthy

When designing the accommodations at Surya Plaza Hotel, our philosophy was guided by a simple question: what do travelers truly need for a comfortable and restorative stay? The answer went beyond just a bed and a bathroom. It was about creating functional, private, and thoughtfully appointed spaces. This is particularly evident in the design of our suites.

The Importance of a Private Balcony

A key feature in many of our rooms, especially our suites, is the inclusion of a private balcony. In a city setting, having a personal outdoor space provides a valuable sense of openness. It’s a place to enjoy a morning cup of tea, read a book, or simply observe the city's rhythm without leaving the comfort of your room. For rooms with a view of the Ganga, the balcony becomes a private vantage point to watch the river's timeless flow.

Creating a Separate Living Space

A hotel room can sometimes feel restrictive, especially during longer stays or when traveling with family. Our Junior and Premium Suites were designed to solve this by incorporating a separate living or drawing room. This functional separation provides several benefits:

  • Work-Life Balance: It allows one guest to work at a desk or take a call without disturbing another who may be resting in the bedroom.
  • Social Space: It provides a comfortable area to host a visitor or for family members to gather without congregating in the sleeping area.
  • Enhanced Comfort: With spaces like our 700 sq. ft. Two-Bedroom Suite, this separation creates an apartment-like feel, making it a true home away from home.

Functionality in Every Detail

Our design process also focused on practical amenities that cater to the needs of modern travelers. This includes:

  • Ample Lighting and Power Outlets: Well-lit spaces with conveniently located outlets for charging devices.
  • Dedicated Workspaces: Rooms are equipped with a desk and chair, recognizing the needs of our business guests.
  • Quality Furnishings: From plush bedding to comfortable sofas, every piece is chosen for both durability and comfort.

By focusing on these core principles—private outdoor space, functional separation, and thoughtful details—we aim to provide an environment that is not just a place to stay, but a space to live comfortably.
