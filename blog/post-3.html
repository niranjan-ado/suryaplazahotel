Designing for Comfort: The Making of Our Suites

Published on September 11, 2025 by Niranjan Ramamurthy

When designing the accommodations at Surya Plaza Hotel, our philosophy was guided by a simple question: what do travelers truly need for a comfortable and restorative stay? The answer went beyond just a bed and a bathroom. It was about creating functional, private, and thoughtfully appointed spaces that feel less like a temporary room and more like a personal sanctuary. This is particularly evident in the design of our suites.

The Importance of a Private Balcony

A key feature in many of our rooms, and a standard in our suites, is the inclusion of a private balcony. In a city setting, having a personal outdoor space provides a valuable sense of openness and a connection to the local environment. Itâ€™s a place to enjoy a morning cup of tea while observing the city's rhythm, or to unwind in the evening air. For our suites with a view of the Ganga, the balcony becomes a private vantage point to watch the river's timeless flow, transforming the room into a more immersive experience.

Creating a Separate Living Space

A standard hotel room can sometimes feel restrictive, especially during longer stays or when traveling with family. Our Junior and Premium Suites were designed to address this by incorporating a separate living or drawing room. This functional separation provides several clear benefits:

  • Work-Life Balance: It allows one guest to work at the dedicated desk or take a call without disturbing another who may be resting in the bedroom.
  • Social Space: It provides a comfortable and appropriate area to host a visitor or for family members to gather without congregating in the sleeping area.
  • Enhanced Comfort: With spaces like our 700 sq. ft. Two-Bedroom Suite, this separation creates an apartment-like feel, making it a true home away from home.

Functionality for an Extended Stay

Our design process also focused on practical amenities that cater to the needs of modern travelers, especially those on longer trips. This includes:

  • Thoughtful Amenities: Conveniences like a mini-fridge and a tea & coffee maker are standard, allowing guests to feel more at home.
  • Ample Storage: Well-designed wardrobes and closets ensure that guests can unpack and settle in, rather than living out of a suitcase.
  • The Kitchen Advantage: In our Family Apartment, the inclusion of a full kitchen offers the ultimate flexibility, allowing guests to prepare their own meals according to their own schedule and preferences.
