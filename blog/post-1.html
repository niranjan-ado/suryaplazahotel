A Weekend Guide to Sahibganj's Hidden Gems

Published on September 11, 2025 by Niranjan Ramamurthy

Sahibganj, a district rich in natural beauty and history, offers a unique blend of scenic landscapes and cultural landmarks. Situated along the Ganga, it provides a compelling destination for a weekend of exploration. This guide outlines a practical itinerary to help you experience some of the key attractions in the region, using Surya Plaza Hotel as your base.

Day 1: Waterfalls and Temples

Morning: Moti Jharna Waterfall

Begin your day with a trip to Moti Jharna, a waterfall located within the Rajmahal hills. The falls cascade over two distinct rock ledges, creating a serene environment, particularly during the post-monsoon season. The surrounding area is characterized by lush greenery, offering a quiet space for nature photography or simply enjoying the calm.

  • Location: Approx. 14 km from Surya Plaza Hotel.
  • Travel Time: About a 35-minute drive.
  • Note: The flow of water is seasonal and is most prominent from July to October.

Afternoon: Shivgadi Dham Temple

After visiting the waterfall, proceed to Shivgadi Dham. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is notable for its location within a natural cave. A unique feature is the continuous dripping of water from the mountain onto the Shiva Lingam. This site holds significant religious importance for devotees in the region.

Day 2: History and Local Life

Morning: The Monuments of Rajmahal

Dedicate the morning to exploring the historical remnants of Rajmahal, which served as the capital of Bengal during the Mughal era in the 16th century. Key sites include:

  • Akbari Masjid: One of the older mosques in the area, showcasing Mughal architectural elements.
  • Singhi Dalan: The remains of Sultan Shah Shujaâ€™s palace, offering a glimpse into the region's royal past.

Afternoon: Sahibganj Ghati and Local Markets

Conclude your weekend with a visit to the Sahibganj Ghati, a river port on the Ganga. This area is significant for local trade and transportation and provides expansive views of the river. Afterwards, you can explore the nearby Chowk Bazar to observe local life and browse for regional goods.
